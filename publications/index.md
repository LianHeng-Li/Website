  <div class="publication-content">
    <h2 class="publication-title">Nitrogen Dominates Global Atmospheric Organic Aerosol Absorption</h2>
    <p class="publication-authors">Yumin Li, Tsung-May Fu, Jian Zhen Yu, Aoxing Zhang, Xu Yu, Jianhuai Ye, Lei Zhu, Huizhong Shen, Chen Wang, Xin Yang, Shu Tao, Qi Chen, Ying Li, Lei Li, Huizheng Che, Colette L. Heald</p>
    <p class="publication-citation"><em>Science</em>, 2025, 387, 6737, 989-995.</p>
    <p class="publication-links"><a href="{{ '/papers/Science%202025%20(%E7%8B%AC%E7%AB%8B%E4%B8%80%E4%BD%9C).pdf' | relative_url }}">Link 1: PDF</a></p>
    <ul class="key-findings">
      <li>BrN contributes 76% of organic aerosol light absorption over the U.S. and 61% globally, with biomass burning as the largest source.</li>
      <li>BrN absorptive capacity decreases substantially with aging, with strong seasonal and spatial variability.</li>
      <li>BrN accounts for 18% of the global absorptive direct radiative effect of carbonaceous aerosols, with a mean radiative forcing of 0.034 W/m^2.</li>
      <li>The study provides a nitrogen-focused framework for assessing the climate impacts of organic aerosols and their chemical evolution.</li>
    </ul>

  <div class="publication-content">
    <h2 class="publication-title">Dissecting the Contributions of Organic Nitrogen Aerosols to Global Atmospheric Nitrogen Deposition and Implications for Ecosystems</h2>
    <p class="publication-authors">Yumin Li, Tzung-May Fu, Jian Zhen Yu, Xu Yu, Qi Chen, Ruqian Miao, Yang Zhou, Aoxing Zhang, Jianhuai Ye, Xin Yang, Shu Tao, Hongbin Liu, Weiqi Yao</p>
    <p class="publication-citation"><em>National Science Review</em>, 2023.</p>
    <p class="publication-links"><a href="{{ '/papers/National%20Science%20Review%20(%E7%8B%AC%E7%AB%8B%E4%B8%80%E4%BD%9C).pdf' | relative_url }}">Link 1: PDF</a></p>
    <ul class="key-findings">
      <li>Global atmospheric organic nitrogen deposition is estimated at 26 Tg N yr<sup>-1</sup>, predominantly in particulate form.</li>
      <li>Wildfires, oceans, and aqueous production are the dominant sources of particulate organic nitrogen deposition.</li>
      <li>Organic nitrogen contributes 40% to 80% of total nitrogen deposition downwind of biomass-burning regions.</li>
      <li>Atmospheric organic nitrogen deposition is a major external nitrogen supply to N-limited boreal forests, tundras, and the Arctic Ocean.</li>
    </ul>

    <h2 class="publication-title">Impacts of Chemical Degradation on the Global Budget of Atmospheric Levoglucosan and Its Use As a Biomass Burning Tracer</h2>
    <p class="publication-authors">Yumin Li, Tzung-May Fu, Jian Zhen Yu, Xu Feng, Lijuan Zhang, Jing Chen, Suresh Kumar Reddy Boreddy, Kimitaka Kawamura, Pingqing Fu, Xin Yang, Lei Zhu, Zhenzhong Zeng</p>
    <p class="publication-citation"><em>Environmental Science &amp; Technology</em>, 2021, 55, 5525-5536.</p>
    <p class="publication-links"><a href="{{ '/papers/EST%202021(%E7%8B%AC%E7%AB%8B%E4%B8%80%E4%BD%9C).pdf' | relative_url }}">Link 1: PDF</a></p>
    <ul class="key-findings">
      <li>The work developed the first global simulation of atmospheric levoglucosan with explicit chemical degradation.</li>
      <li>Accounting for degradation is necessary to reproduce the observed decline of levoglucosan from source to remote regions.</li>
      <li>The estimated global atmospheric burden of levoglucosan is 19 Gg with an average lifetime of 1.8 days.</li>
      <li>The study provides statistical parameterizations that improve the use of levoglucosan measurements in biomass-burning source apportionment.</li>
    </ul>
